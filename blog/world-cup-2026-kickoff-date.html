The Historic 48-Team Expansion Begins

Watch every kickoff live, featuring more matches, host countries, and stadiums than ever before!

The countdown is officially ticking! The FIFA World Cup 2026 is poised to be the largest, most spectacular sporting event in human history. For the first time ever, the tournament will expand to feature 48 competing nations across three host nations: the United States, Mexico, and Canada.

This expansion brings an unprecedented total of 104 matches (up from 64 in the traditional format), creating a month-long celebration of pure football energy. Here is everything you need to know about the official kickoff, key host cities, and tournament groups.

📅 Official Kickoff Date & Locations

The official kickoff of the FIFA World Cup 2026 is scheduled for June 11, 2026. The historic opening match will take place in Mexico City at the legendary Estadio Azteca—a temple of football history that has previously hosted matches played by legends Pele and Diego Maradona.

Meanwhile, the final match of the tournament will be played on July 19, 2026, at the state-of-the-art MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey (New York / New Jersey Area).

"The Estadio Azteca in Mexico City will host the opening match of the 2026 World Cup, marking the third time this iconic venue has hosted a World Cup opener, while New Jersey's MetLife Stadium secures the final showdown."
